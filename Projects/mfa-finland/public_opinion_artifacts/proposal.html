1. Strategic Context

Every embassy in the world operates under the same five core functions defined by the Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations (1961, Article 3):

#Core FunctionDescription
1Represent IranBe the official face of Iran in Finland
2Protect nationalsServe Iranian citizens living in or traveling to Finland
3NegotiateMaintain diplomatic channels with the Finnish government
4Observe & reportMonitor conditions in Finland, report to Tehran
5Promote friendly relationsBuild economic, cultural, and scientific ties
Key insight: Function #5 — "Promote friendly relations" — is the only mandate that is public-facing and where external support can make a real, measurable difference. This proposal focuses entirely on this function.

Why This Matters Now

Finland–Iran bilateral relations are at a critical point:

  • Finland's travel advisory for Iran is at the highest level: "Avoid all travel" (since Oct 2025).
  • The Finnish FM summoned the ambassador to protest domestic crackdowns.
  • Bilateral trade has dropped to single-digit millions (USD) — effectively frozen.
  • No major cultural cooperation events have been documented in 2025.

In this environment, cultural diplomacy and citizen services are the embassy's most powerful tools to maintain relevance, build goodwill, and create positive bilateral momentum.

3. Proposed Initiatives

Each initiative maps directly to Function #5 (Promote Friendly Relations) of the embassy's mandate.

Initiative 1: "Bridge of Culture" Program

Cultural Diplomacy Medium Effort High Impact Low Cost

A recurring series of small-scale cultural events targeting both the Finnish public and the Iranian diaspora:

  • Nowruz Open House (March) — invite Finnish neighbors, media, local politicians
  • Persian Film Night (quarterly) — partner with Finnish cultural centres
  • Language & Calligraphy Workshops — simple, high-engagement, low-cost
  • Academic Partnerships — connect Iranian researchers at Finnish universities with the embassy

Initiative 2: Digital Consular Modernization

Citizen Services Low Effort Very High Impact Very Low Cost

A standalone landing page — independent of the broken MFA website — with:

  • Clear consular FAQs in Farsi, English, and Finnish
  • Simple appointment booking form
  • Direct contact form (free — no premium phone required)

This bypasses the centrally-controlled MFA system entirely. The embassy maintains full control.

Initiative 3: Social Media Diversification

Public Relations Low Effort Medium Impact Zero Cost
  • Shift X/Twitter content to a 60/40 split — 60% local bilateral content, 40% MFA policy
  • Launch Instagram account focused on visual cultural content for the diaspora and Finnish public
  • Establish LinkedIn page — see Initiative 5 below

Initiative 5: LinkedIn for Trade & Institutional Positioning

Economic Diplomacy Low Effort High Impact Low Cost

Create an official LinkedIn page for the embassy, positioned as the professional channel for economic and academic engagement:

  • Trade & Investment — Highlight bilateral trade opportunities, Finnish companies active in Iran, and Iranian businesses in Finland
  • Academic & Research — Feature Iranian researchers at Finnish universities, joint research programs, and scholarship opportunities
  • Sustainability & Climate — Leverage the ambassador's UNFCCC background to publish thought leadership on Finland-Iran environmental cooperation
  • Event Amplification — Cross-promote embassy cultural events to a professional audience

LinkedIn is where Finland's business community, academics, and institutional decision-makers are most active. A dedicated presence positions the embassy as a serious partner for bilateral economic and scientific cooperation — directly serving the embassy's core mandate.

Content cadence: 1–2 posts per week. Can be fully outsourced.

Initiative 4: Finnish Media Engagement

Narrative Control Medium Effort Very High Impact Low Cost
  • Draft and distribute op-eds in Finnish media on cultural topics
  • Invite Finnish journalists to embassy cultural events
  • Prepare response statements for common media inquiries

Currently, the embassy has zero counter-narrative in Finnish media. Every story is told by others.
