1. Executive Summary

This document maps 35+ Finnish organizations, institutions, and individuals that can serve as strategic partners for the Embassy of the Islamic Republic of Iran in Helsinki. Each stakeholder is categorized by type, relevance, and potential partnership model.

Based on this mapping, we propose four complementary event series — each targeting a different audience and strategic objective:

StrategyFocusPrimary AudienceAmbassador Fit
🎬 Persia & FinlandCinema, music, artFinnish public, diasporaGood
🕊️ Bridge BuildersDialogue, international lawAcademics, diplomats, mediaStrongest
🌱 Green BridgeClimate & sustainabilityProfessionals, institutionsVery strong
💼 Business BridgeTrade & innovationEntrepreneurs, businessesGood
Strategic principle: Every event must position Iran as a civilization and partner — not as a geopolitical issue. The goal is to create positive, firsthand experiences that Finnish audiences share organically.

Context: Why Now?

  • Finland–Iran relations are at a historic low (highest travel advisory, frozen trade, diplomatic friction).
  • The ambassador's stated mandate is to "expand bilateral relations across various fields."
  • There are ~24,000 Persian speakers and ~13,600 Iranian-born residents in Finland — a significant diaspora community.
  • An "Iranian Culture Festival" was successfully held at Kino Konepaja in February 2025 — proving there is audience demand.
  • Finland's global leadership in sustainability, education, and peace mediation creates natural cooperation angles that transcend politics.

2. Iranian Diaspora & Community Organizations

These organizations are the backbone of the Iranian community in Finland. Partnership with them provides immediate reach and community trust.

KOPE ry — Kotoutumisen ja persiankielisten tuki
NGO — Integration & community support | Helsinki

The primary support organization for Persian-speaking communities in Finland. Provides integration guidance, social advisory services, Finnish and Farsi language courses, and community programming.

Partnership potential: Co-host community events, diaspora outreach, Nowruz programming, direct channel to Iranian families.

🔗 moniheli.fi/jasenyhdistykset/kope-ry

Persian Business Association in Finland (PBAF)
Business network — Non-profit | Helsinki

Professional platform connecting Persian-speaking entrepreneurs with the Finnish business ecosystem. Hosts networking events, market entry support, business consulting, and training programs.

Partnership potential: Co-host business mixers, LinkedIn content partner, trade promotion events, speaker sourcing.

🔗 pbaf.fi

Moniheli
Umbrella network — 180+ multicultural associations | Helsinki

Finland's largest multicultural network. Supports immigrant integration, equity, and social inclusion. KOPE ry is a member organization.

Partnership potential: Distribution network, event co-promotion, access to broader multicultural community.

🔗 moniheli.fi

Baran Band
Music ensemble — Persian & Kurdish music | Helsinki (est. 2013)

Performs compositions and arrangements of traditional Persian and Kurdish music. Helsinki-based, active in the Finnish cultural scene.

Partnership potential: Live performances at embassy cultural events, Nowruz, film nights.

🔗 aineetonkulttuuriperinto.fi (Finnish intangible cultural heritage registry)

Iranian Student Associations (Aalto, Helsinki, Tampere)
Student organizations — Universities across Finland

Active student groups at major universities. Organize cultural events, music performances, and networking within campus communities.

Partnership potential: Student engagement, campus events, ambassador guest lectures, youth outreach.

🔗 ayy.fi/associations (Aalto Student Union directory)

3. Academic Institutions

Academic partnerships offer long-term credibility and create a pipeline of informed Finnish professionals who understand Iran beyond the headlines.

University of Helsinki — Middle Eastern Studies
Academic — Faculty of Humanities | Helsinki

Finland's primary academic hub for Middle Eastern studies. Offers Persian language courses, area studies programs within the Bachelor's Programme in Languages and Master's Programme in Area and Cultural Studies. Covers Arabic, Hebrew, Turkish, and Persian.

Partnership potential: Ambassador guest lectures, co-hosted seminars, student visits to embassy, research collaboration.

🔗 helsinki.fi — Middle Eastern Studies

FIME — Finnish Institute in the Middle East
Research institute — Helsinki + Middle East offices

Dedicated research institute studying the region's languages, cultures, religions, and societies. Facilitates academic cooperation between Finland and the Middle East. Provides research grants. Already runs Iran-focused events (e.g., April 2026 event on "Iran's opposition — from revolution to today").

Partnership potential: Key academic partner. Co-organize seminars, leverage their event infrastructure, access their researcher network.

🔗 fime.fi

Tampere University — MENA Research
Academic — Research groups | Tampere

Hosts MENA-focused research groups and organizes the Finnish Colloquium of Middle East and North African Studies. No standalone Persian program, but active in regional studies research.

Partnership potential: Academic conference participation, research partnerships, satellite events outside Helsinki.

🔗 tuni.fi

Helsinki Adult Education Centre (Helsingin aikuisopisto)
Adult education — Language courses | Helsinki

Offers Persian language courses from beginner to advanced, introducing students to Persian culture alongside language skills.

Partnership potential: Cross-promote embassy cultural events to Persian language students — a warm, self-selecting audience.

🔗 helao.fi

4. Peace, Dialogue & Mediation NGOs

Finland is a global leader in peace mediation. Partnering with these organizations transforms the embassy's positioning from "controversial diplomatic outpost" to "constructive dialogue partner."

CMI — Martti Ahtisaari Peace Foundation
Peace & conflict resolution — Independent foundation | Helsinki

Founded by Nobel Peace Prize laureate and former President Martti Ahtisaari. A global leader in conflict prevention and resolution through informal dialogue and mediation. Active in the Middle East, North Africa, Eurasia, Sub-Saharan Africa, and Asia.

Partnership potential: Highest-value partner. A single co-hosted dialogue event with CMI changes the embassy's entire public positioning. The ambassador's international law background aligns perfectly with their mission.

🔗 cmi.fi

Finnish Forum for Mediation (Suomen sovittelufoorumi)
Mediation advocacy — National organization

Promotes mediation at national and international levels. Focuses on creating communication channels and reducing violence for sustainable peace.

Partnership potential: Dialogue-themed events, institutional credibility for embassy programming.

🔗 sovittelu.com

Network for Religious and Traditional Peacemakers
International peacebuilding network — Supported by Finn Church Aid | Helsinki

Focuses on the role of religious and traditional actors in peacebuilding. Convenes in Helsinki regularly. Supported by Finland's Ministry for Foreign Affairs.

Partnership potential: Interfaith dialogue events, cultural bridge-building, reaching Finnish faith communities.

🔗 peacemakersnetwork.org

Ad Astra
Interfaith & intercultural dialogue — Helsinki

Focuses on interfaith and intercultural dialogue, antiracism, and promotion of cultural heritage.

Partnership potential: Cultural heritage events, intercultural dialogue programming.

🔗 eifd.eu (European Institute for Freedom and Dialogue)

Global Dignity Finland
Cross-cultural dialogue — Helsinki

Works on diversity and inclusion through workshops and educational initiatives.

Partnership potential: Workshop-style events, school outreach, youth engagement.

🔗 globaldignity.org

5. Public Offices & City Infrastructure

These are the venues, funding sources, and institutional platforms that give events official weight and public legitimacy.

Multicultural cultural center — City of Helsinki | Kaisaniemenkatu 8

Helsinki's dedicated multicultural arts hub. Hosts exhibitions, performances, events, and workshops for diverse cultural communities. Managed by the City of Helsinki Culture and Leisure Division.

Partnership potential: Primary venue for embassy cultural events. Purpose-built for this kind of programming. City endorsement included.

🔗 caisa.fi

City of Helsinki — Culture and Leisure Division
Municipal government — Cultural policy & grants

Manages Helsinki's cultural centers, museums, and arts initiatives. Distributes approximately €18 million annually in cultural grants, including projects enhancing cultural diversity.

Partnership potential: Grant applications for cultural events, venue access, city-level endorsement.

🔗 hel.fi/kulttuuri-ja-vapaa-aika

Stoa / Malmitalo / Kanneltalo / Vuotalo
Regional cultural centers — City of Helsinki

Cultural centers across Helsinki neighborhoods hosting concerts, theater, film screenings, and exhibitions.

Partnership potential: Satellite venue options to reach audiences outside city center.

🔗 hel.fi — Cultural centers

Finland's Ministry for Foreign Affairs — MENA Strategy
Government — Development cooperation

Coordinates Finland's development cooperation with the MENA region (2021–2024 strategy). Priorities include climate action, sustainable growth, SME support, and a "just green transition." Uses a "Team Finland" approach integrating private sector expertise.

Partnership potential: Institutional backing for sustainability/climate cooperation events. Aligns with ambassador's UNFCCC background.

🔗 um.fi — MENA Development Cooperation

6. Media & Cultural Venues

Kino Konepaja
Independent art-house cinema & bar | Vallila, Helsinki

Intimate cinema hosting screenings, film clubs, and cultural events. Successfully hosted the "Iranian Culture Festival" in February 2025 — an all-day event with film screenings (My Favourite Cake, The Seed of the Sacred Fig), panel discussions, and Persian music.

Partnership potential: Proven partner. The 2025 event validated audience demand. A recurring "Persian Cinema Night" series is the obvious next step.

🔗 kinokonepaja.fi

Tampere Film Festival
International short film festival | Tampere

One of Europe's leading short film festivals. Has a long history of featuring Iranian cinema. In 2025, the Tehran International Short Film Festival curated a special program "Pioneers: Iranian Echoes from Tampere" featuring award-winning films from Tampere.

Partnership potential: Official embassy presence at the festival, sponsor an Iranian film program, connect Iranian filmmakers.

🔗 tamperefilmfestival.fi

Helsingin Sanomat / Yle / Helsinki Times
Major Finnish media outlets

Helsingin Sanomat — Finland's largest newspaper (print + digital).
Yle — National public broadcaster (TV, radio, digital).
Helsinki Times — English-language newspaper for international/expat audience.

Partnership potential: Op-ed placement, cultural event coverage, interview opportunities. Currently, the embassy has zero counter-narrative in Finnish media.

🔗 hs.fi · yle.fi · helsinkitimes.fi

Savoy Theatre
International concert hall | Helsinki city center

Premium venue for high-profile performances and guest events.

Partnership potential: Major cultural gala events (e.g., annual Nowruz celebration with diplomatic guests).

🔗 savoyteatteri.fi

Target Audience

Sustainability professionals, environmental researchers, institutional partners, Finnish media.

Frequency & Venue
