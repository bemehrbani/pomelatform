# Corporate Knowledge Base: Finland Startup Residence Permit Extension Guide

*Last Updated: July 2026*

This document serves as the authoritative compliance and operational guide for international founders navigating the extension of a **Startup Residence Permit** in Finland. It details the legal frameworks of the Finnish Immigration Service (Migri) and the Centre for Economic Development, Transport and the Environment (ELY Centre), outlines critical timelines, and maps the primary reasons for application failures.

---

## 1. Overview of the Finnish Startup Residence Permit Lifecycle

A Finnish Startup Residence Permit is issued to non-EU/EEA founders who intend to build an innovative, high-growth startup in Finland. The lifecycle generally progresses as follows:

```
                  ┌────────────────────────────────────────┐
                  │       FIRST RESIDENCE PERMIT           │
                  │   • Vetted by Business Finland         │
                  │   • Proof of €1,000/mo personal funds  │
                  │   • Valid for up to 2 years (A-Permit) │
                  └───────────────────┬────────────────────┘
                                      │
                                      ▼
                  ┌────────────────────────────────────────┐
                  │       EXTENDED PERMIT APPLICATION      │
                  │   • Vetted directly by Migri           │
                  │   • Bypasses ELY Centre partial dec.   │
                  │   • Vetted on progress & Oy setup      │
                  └───────────────────┬────────────────────┘
                                      │
                         ┌────────────┴────────────┐
                         ▼                         ▼
             [ COMPLIANT (PRH + Income) ]   [ NON-COMPLIANT (Pivot / Def) ]
             • Extended for up to 4 years   • Forced to pivot to Entrepreneur path
             • Eligible for PR after 4 yrs  • Subject to ELY Centre profitability
```

1.  **First Residence Permit:** Valid for up to **2 years**. Vetted initially by **Business Finland** for eligibility, and finalized by Migri.
2.  **Extended Residence Permit:** Applied for *before* the first permit expires (ideally 2 months prior). Valid for up to **4 years**.
3.  **Permanent Residence (PR):** Eligible after **4 years** of continuous residence in Finland on an "A-permit" (which is the permit class assigned to both startup and general entrepreneurs).

## 2. Key Differences in Agency Roles

Navigating the extension requires understanding which agency is responsible for what decision:

### 1. Business Finland
*   **First Permit Role:** Issues the mandatory **Eligibility Statement** (valid for 2 months) evaluating the innovation and growth potential of the startup.
*   **Extension Role:** **Bypassed.** You do **not** need a new statement from Business Finland to apply for an extended startup permit.

### 2. Finnish Immigration Service (Migri)
*   **First Permit & Extension Role:** The final decision-maker. For extensions, Migri evaluates the application directly (bypassing the ELY Centre) if you apply under the "Startup Entrepreneur" category. 
*   **Evaluation Factors:** Migri verifies the existence of the company, operational progress, physical presence in Finland, and secured means of support.

### 3. ELY Centre (Centre for Economic Development, Transport and the Environment)
*   **Role in Startup Permits:** **None.** Bypassed during both first and extended startup permit applications.
*   **Role in General Entrepreneur Permits:** **Mandatory.** If a startup founder pivots their business to a traditional company (consultancy, restaurant, or agency), they must apply for the extension under the general "Entrepreneur" category. The ELY Centre must issue a **partial decision** evaluating the company's profitability, business plan, and financial projections.

## 3. Detailed Criteria for Startup Extension Success

To secure an extended permit under the startup category, the applicant must meet five core requirements:

### 1. Limited Company Incorporation (Osakeyhtiö)
You must have successfully incorporated your company as an *Osakeyhtiö* (Oy) in Finland. 
*   **Requirement:** The company must be active, registered in the Trade Register (PRH), and possess a valid **Business ID** (*Y-tunnus*). 
*   **Pitfall:** Founders who fail to register their Oy within the first two years (often due to banking delays or co-founder disputes) will have their extension application rejected.

### 2. Operational Business Progress
You must prove that you have actively worked on the business plan approved during your initial statement.
*   **Evidence Required:** 
    *   Updated business plans, product roadmaps, or technical designs.
    *   Customer contracts, Pilot Agreements, Letters of Intent (LOIs).
    *   Bookkeeping records, corporate bank statements, or proof of seed funding raised.

### 3. Secured Livelihood (Income Requirement)
For the extension, Migri expects you to support yourself without relying on Finnish social benefits. This is verified using the Incomes Register (*Tulorekisteri*).
*   **Required Livelihood Thresholds (Net Monthly Income, 2026):**
    *   *Southern Finland (Helsinki / Uusimaa):* **€1,210 / month**
    *   *Central Finland:* **€1,090 / month**
    *   *Northern Finland:* **€1,030 / month**
*   **How to Prove Livelihood:**
    *   A regular salary paid to you by your Finnish Oy (evidenced by payroll slips and tax records).
    *   Personal funds (bank accounts showing cash deposits or savings).
    *   Other secure income (investments, capital gains, or spousal support).

### 4. Mandatory YEL Insurance (Self-Employed Pension)
Under Finnish law, any entrepreneur working in their own company must buy **YEL (Self-Employed Persons' Pension Insurance)** if they meet the minimum criteria:
*   **Applicability:** The entrepreneur is aged 18–67, resides in Finland, has worked in their company for at least 4 continuous months, and has a work input valued above the minimum threshold.
*   **YEL Income Threshold (2025/2026):** Minimum of **€9,200+ per year** (set by Eläketurvakeskus).
*   **Migri Check:** Migri checks if your YEL insurance is paid and active. Having unpaid YEL premiums or failing to register is a primary cause of permit rejection.

### 5. Physical Presence in Finland
The Startup Permit is granted to build a business *in* Finland, not to run it remotely from abroad.
*   **Requirement:** You must reside in Finland. You must have spent **less than half of the permit's duration abroad**. If you spend more than 50% of your time traveling or residing in your home country, Migri will deny your extension on the grounds that your actual center of life is not in Finland.

## 4. Rejection Scenarios & Remediation Playbook

If a startup permit extension is rejected, the consequences are immediate. The rejection typically includes a **removal order** (deportation) from Finland and a 30-day voluntary departure window.

### Common Rejection Triggers:
1.  **No Salary/Funds:** The startup is pre-revenue, has raised zero capital, and the founder's bank balance is near zero.
2.  **The Pivot Trap:** The startup transitioned to a local service agency. The founder applied for an extension, but Migri routed the file to the ELY Centre under "Entrepreneur" rules. The ELY Centre issued a negative partial decision because the agency had low profits or could not guarantee a €1,210/month salary.
3.  **Physical Absence:** The founder traveled constantly to secure clients and spent 14 months of the 24-month permit duration outside Finland.

### Step-by-Step Appeal and Remediation Playbook:

```
 ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │                      REJECTION OF EXTENDED PERMIT                      │
 └───────────────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────────┘
                                     │
                                     ▼
 ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ 1. ENGAGE MIGRATION LEGAL COUNSEL                                      │
 │    • Review the written grounds of rejection.                          │
 │    • Evaluate state-funded legal aid eligibility.                      │
 └───────────────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────────┘
                                     │
                                     ▼
 ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ 2. FILE AN APPEAL (ADMINISTRATIVE COURT)                               │
 │    • Submit appeal to Helsinki Administrative Court within 30 days.    │
 │    • Submit a separate "Suspension of Enforcement" request.            │
 └───────────────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────────┘
                                     │
                                     ▼
 ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
 │ 3. SECURE INTERIM LEGAL STATUS                                         │
 │    • A registered appeal allows you to remain in Finland legally.      │
 │    • Continue business operations while awaiting the court decision.   │
 └────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

1.  **Engage Migration Counsel:** Immediately consult an immigration lawyer or specialized legal service (such as Lexia growth lawyers). Check if you qualify for state-funded legal aid to cover attorney costs.
2.  **File an Appeal to the Administrative Court:** You have a strict deadline of **30 days** from the receipt of the decision to submit an appeal to the Administrative Court (*hallinto-oikeus*).
3.  **Request Suspension of Enforcement:** You must submit a separate petition for the court to suspend the enforcement of your removal order. If granted, this allows you to legally remain in Finland and continue running your business while the appeal is processed.
4.  **Evaluate Alternative Permits:** If the business cannot be saved, check if you qualify for a different residence permit (e.g., a work-based permit if you secure a local job contract, or a job-seeking permit if you hold a Finnish university degree).

## 5. Upcoming Legislative Reforms (2026–2028)

The Finnish Ministry of Economic Affairs and Employment is actively reforming the startup permit framework to streamline administration. A draft proposal went for public consultation on May 27, 2026.

### Expected Reform Outcomes (Early 2028 Implementation):
*   **Removal of the Free Advance Statement:** The current separate, free Business Finland statement will be eliminated.
*   **Integrated Paid Application:** The business assessment will be conducted directly inside Migri’s paid permit application process.
*   **Post-Permit Business ID:** Future applicants can apply for a Finnish Business ID *after* receiving their permit, solving a major incorporation bottleneck for foreign nationals.
